🌍 ESG Climate & Water Risk Analysis for Investment Portfolios

A comprehensive Python-based framework for analyzing climate transition risk and water scarcity exposure in investment portfolios

📊 Project Overview

This project develops a Python-based ESG analytics framework to estimate an investment portfolio's exposure to climate transition risk and water scarcity risk. While many ESG risk models focus primarily on carbon emissions, this analysis introduces a dual-risk framework that integrates both carbon intensity and water stress exposure.

The objective is to demonstrate how environmental risk data can be integrated into portfolio risk analysis, helping investors better understand sustainability-related exposures and make more informed allocation decisions.

The project highlights how water scarcity, an often overlooked environmental risk, can significantly affect sectors such as beverages, agriculture, utilities, and consumer goods, making it an important factor in sustainable investment analysis.

🎯 Project Objectives

The project aims to:

  1. Estimate carbon transition risk exposure of an investment portfolio
  2. Measure water stress exposure across sectors and holdings
  3. Identify sectors and companies contributing most to environmental risk
  4. Calculate weighted environmental exposure based on portfolio allocation
  5. Visualise how environmental risk is distributed across the portfolio
  6. Evaluate how portfolio rebalancing can reduce sustainability risk
  7. Demonstrate how ESG data can support more resilient investment decisions

📁 Dataset Description

The analysis uses three datasets:

Portfolio Dataset

A simulated investment portfolio containing 30 companies across 8 sectors.

Column Description
company Company name
sector Industry sector classification
portfolio_weight Portfolio allocation weight

Sector Risk Dataset

Sector-level environmental intensity metrics used as proxies for environmental exposure.

Column Description
sector Industry sector
carbon_intensity Estimated sector carbon intensity
water_intensity Estimated sector water intensity

Water Stress Dataset

Regional water scarcity indicators used to approximate geographic water risk.

Column Description
region Operating region
water_stress_index Regional water scarcity score

🔬 Methodology

The analysis follows these steps:

  1. Load portfolio, sector intensity, and water stress datasets
  2. Merge portfolio holdings with environmental intensity metrics
  3. Calculate weighted carbon and water exposure for each company
  4. Aggregate environmental exposure by company and sector
  5. Visualize portfolio sustainability risk distribution
  6. Perform scenario analysis through portfolio rebalancing
  7. Simulate environmental cost impacts under carbon and water pricing scenarios

📐 Key Formulas

Carbon Exposure:
carbon_exposure = portfolio_weight × carbon_intensity
Water Exposure:
water_exposure = portfolio_weight × water_intensity × water_stress_index
Composite ESG Exposure:
composite_esg = carbon_exposure + (0.6 × water_exposure)
Carbon Cost Scenario:
carbon_cost = carbon_exposure × carbon_price
Water Scarcity Cost Scenario:
water_cost = water_exposure × water_price

📈 Visualizations

The project generates five key visual outputs:

  1. Sector ESG Exposure - Stacked bar chart showing combined carbon and water exposure across sectors.
  2. Portfolio Allocation - Donut chart illustrating the distribution of portfolio weight by sector.
  3. Top 10 Holdings by Environmental Risk - Horizontal bar chart highlighting the companies contributing most to total ESG exposure.
  4. Portfolio Rebalancing Comparison - Side-by-side comparison showing how reallocating toward lower-risk sectors reduces carbon and water exposure.
  5. Carbon vs Water Risk Scatter Plot - Bubble chart visualizing holdings by carbon exposure (x-axis), water exposure (y-axis), and portfolio weight (bubble size), identifying companies in the high-risk quadrant.

💡 Key Insights

The analysis reveals several important sustainability insights:

  • Environmental exposure is often concentrated in a few high-intensity sectors
  • Carbon and water risks are not always correlated, meaning carbon-focused ESG strategies may overlook significant environmental risks
  • Water-intensive sectors such as beverages, agriculture, and utilities can face significant operational risk under water scarcity
  • Portfolio rebalancing toward lower-intensity sectors can meaningfully reduce environmental risk exposure
  • Integrating multiple environmental metrics can support more robust ESG investment strategies

🎓 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how carbon and water risk data can be integrated into portfolio analytics to identify sustainability-related exposures. By combining portfolio allocation data with environmental intensity metrics, investors can better understand how climate transition risk and water scarcity may affect their investments.

The analysis highlights the importance of multi-dimensional ESG frameworks that extend beyond carbon emissions and incorporate broader resource risks that may shape the future of sustainable finance.
